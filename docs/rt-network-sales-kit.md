# RT Network — Sales Kit

*For pitching venues, vendors and crew as founding members. Companion: [rt-network-pricing.md](rt-network-pricing.md).*

---

## The one-liner

> **"Confirmed, pre-checked bookings delivered to your inbox — you keep 98 cents on the euro."**

## Positioning (never open with technology)

You are not selling software. You are selling **booked gigs with the chaos removed**. The tech (deterministic pre-flight, the concierge, D1) is *proof*, not pitch. Lead with what lands in their inbox: a request that already has a date, a guest count, a budget that clears, gear that doesn't conflict, and a client who has already seen the price.

## The 30-second pitch — three audiences

**To a venue (Lilium, FluxBau):**
> "Clients build their own event package on our site — your space, real capacity limits, live prices. Before you ever see a request, our engine has checked it: guest count fits your room, budget clears, nothing double-booked. You open a link, see the gig and your payout, and tap Confirm. We take 2% as a founding partner — locked for two years. No listing fees, ever."

**To a vendor/crew member:**
> "You get gig requests, not inquiries. Date, hours, headcount, and your exact payout are on the card before you decide. Confirm or decline in ten seconds from your phone. No profile to maintain, no chasing — the network brings the work to you. Founding rate: we keep 2 cents on the euro, only on gigs you actually play."

**To an event client (weddings/corporate):**
> "Plan your event in one conversation — tell us what you're celebrating, slide your budget per guest, and watch a real quote build itself with a draft run of show. Every provider confirms personally before anything is booked. No calls, no chasing caterers."

## The 5-minute live demo (this closes deals — do it on their phone)

1. Open `/#plan` **on their phone**. Type: *"a corporate summer party for 150 people in September, around 8k."* Watch the concierge fill the plan.
2. Slide the catering budget-per-guest down — premium greys out, street-food stays. Say: *"your client self-qualifies before you ever hear from them."*
3. Show the quote + **draft run of show**. Say: *"generated, not promised — same inputs, same schedule, every time."*
4. Open a backstage link (use the demo provider). Show the gig card: gross, **"You receive: €X."** Tap Confirm. Say: *"that's the whole job. Ten seconds."*
5. (Venue people only) Show the Pre-Flight tab: *"this is what runs before you see a request — capacity, budget hard-stops, double-booked gear."*

## Objection handling

| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| *"Your site says no fees, no middleman."* | "The network is still free — listings, introductions, being found cost nothing. The 2% only exists on bookings **we assemble, pre-check and confirm end-to-end**. Introductions free; booked-and-guaranteed carries the fee." |
| *"Why should I pay anything?"* | "You don't pay — the booking does, and only when it happens. Declined gig, cancelled event: zero. Compare 2% to what a no-show client or a double-booked Saturday costs you." |
| *"2% now, what later?"* | "Founding rate is contract-locked for 24 months from your first confirmed booking. Standard members join at 10%. That gap is the reason to sign this month, not next year." |
| *"I get bookings on Instagram already."* | "Keep them — we're not exclusive. This is for the bookings you're *not* getting: clients who plan at 23:00 on their phone and book whoever answers first. We answer first, and we answer with your calendar." |
| *"What about my data / GDPR?"* | "Database is in the EU (Cloudflare, EEUR region). Clients' CRM data stays in their browser. You get a private link — no account, no password to leak." |
| *"What if the client no-shows or won't pay?"* | "Today you invoice the client directly, as you always did — we only invoice our 2% after the event completes. When payments move through the platform (next phase), you'll get deposits held upfront." |

## Closing — pick by temperature

**Warm (venue you know):** the assumptive close.
> "I'll send your backstage link today — the next request that fits your room shows up there. If nothing lands in a month, you've lost nothing and it stays free to be listed."

**Curious but hesitant:** the pilot close.
> "One event. Next fitting request comes to you first; you see the payout before you decide. If confirming a real gig from your phone in ten seconds isn't obviously better, we shake hands and you keep the free listing."

**Price-sensitive:** the founding-scarcity close.
> "The 2% founding rate is for the first partners who help us prove this — it locks for two years the day your first booking confirms. After the founding window, new partners come in at 10%. I'd rather have you at 2."

**Always end with a date, not a maybe:** "Can I send the link now and check in Friday?"

## Proof points (memorize)

- **Live product**, not a deck: real quote builder, real payouts, real confirmations — demo on any phone.
- **Deterministic pre-flight**: capacity, budget hard-stops, staffing coverage, AV conflicts — checked before any provider is contacted. Nobody else in Berlin leads with this.
- **EU data residency** (EEUR); client CRM data never leaves the browser.
- **Real venue data already live**: Lilium's and FluxBau's actual capacity drive the checks; the two share a building, so a combined co-rental is possible for oversized guest counts — capacity for that isn't confirmed yet, so don't quote a number, just flag it as an option.
- **Costs the network ~€0 to run** — the 2% isn't paying for our servers, it's building the network's flywheel.
